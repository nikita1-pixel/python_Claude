# Vision — why this works

Most people "learning to code" drown in one of two ways: a 40-hour video course they never finish, or a pile of tutorials that never connect into the ability to *build something*. This repo is a third path.

## The core bet

**A mentor with memory beats a course with content.**

A great human tutor doesn't hand you a syllabus and walk away. They sit next to you, explain one idea, watch you try it, catch the exact thing you got wrong, and pick up next time exactly where you left off. That's what this turns Claude into — using four simple ideas:

1. **One concept at a time, then build.** Every phase ends in a thing that runs. You don't "learn loops" — you build a number-guessing game and discover you now understand loops.

2. **Verbatim memory.** Every explanation is saved word-for-word in `sessions/`. Summaries lose the teaching; the full transcript is a textbook you wrote yourself, in the order you needed it.

3. **A warm restart, every time.** `memory/study-state.md` holds a resume point. Close the laptop mid-lesson, come back a week later — the mentor opens with *exactly* where you stopped. No re-orientation tax.

4. **Checkpoints that gate progress.** You don't advance on vibes. Each phase ends with a quiz that mixes predict-the-output, spot-the-bug, and explain-it-back. Pass and move on; stumble and the gap gets patched before it compounds.

## The destination

Not "I watched a Python course." The destination is: **you can open a blank file and build the thing** — and you have a GitHub repo of projects to prove it. The 10 phases march deliberately toward that: foundations → data → functions → files → objects → pandas → real tooling → a portfolio project you'd show an interviewer.

## The pace truth

Two focused hours a day, five days a week, gets most people from zero to genuinely job-ready in three to four months. Not because two hours is magic — because *consistency* is. Ten hours one weekend and nothing for three weeks teaches you less than thirty minutes every day. Show up small, show up often.

## The vibe

Keep it geeky. Curiosity is the fuel. Every phase starts with *why this matters in the real world* — because motivation isn't a personality trait, it's a renewable resource you top up by seeing what the skill unlocks.
